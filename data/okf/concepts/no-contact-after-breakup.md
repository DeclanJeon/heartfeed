# Definition

The no-contact rule is a strategy where you completely cut off communication with an ex-partner for a set period (typically 30-90 days) after a breakup. This includes blocking or muting on social media, not responding to messages, and avoiding places where you might run into them.

# Common goals

- Allow emotional healing without reopening wounds
- Break the cycle of dependency and anxious attachment
- Create space for personal growth and reflection
- Reduce the intensity of withdrawal-like symptoms from relationship loss
